Our Mission

At Reading is Magic, our mission is to promote positive reading and creative writing experiences for children and adults through vibrant community events and innovative, educational programs to help inspire, encourage, and motivate families and educators to plug into the power of reading and creative writing.

About Us

Our President and CEO Beverly Melasi-Haag brings a wealth of experience as a dedicated community leader and educator. As a published children’s book author and the former President of the South Florida Writers Association (SWFA), she has been very instrumental in fostering a love for reading and creative writing throughout the South Florida region. Her passion for literacy and commitment to community engagement drives our vision of empowering families, parents, and children to discover the joy and importance of reading.

We believe that literacy is the foundation for a brighter future, and we are committed to making a difference in underserved communities.
‍
Our vision is simple yet profound: to create a community where families, educators, and community members connect through stories, explore the boundless opportunities that books provide, and find their voice through creative writing. We believe in equipping parents and children with the tools they need to engage with their communities through reading and creative writing, nurturing a lifelong love for storytelling and learning.

At Reading is Magic, we are committed to being a positive force in the lives of those we serve, helping build stronger communities, one story at a time.

Empowering Parents: Guiding Your Child’s Reading Adventure

Tips for Parents: How to Foster a Love of Reading

• Create a daily reading routine: Dedicate 15-30 minutes to reading together.
‍
• Be a role model: Let your child see you enjoying books.

• Make reading fun: Use voices, act out scenes, or choose books that match their interests.

• Set reading goals: Encourage your child to track the number of books or chapters they read.

• Ask questions: Discuss the stories, ask about their favorite parts, or what they would change in the story.
